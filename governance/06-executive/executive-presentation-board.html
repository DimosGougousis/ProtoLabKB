Why This Programme Exists

To obtain a DNB license (PSP, EMI, or CASP), any FinTech deploying AI must demonstrate — before its first customer transaction — that its AI systems are sound, accountable, fair, ethical, operated by skilled people, and transparent. DNB evaluates this through the SAFEST framework, which is layered on top of binding statutory requirements from Dutch and EU law.

This programme maps every statutory obligation to a concrete deliverable and assigns it to an implementation phase. The Board's role is to approve the programme, ensure adequate resourcing, and demonstrate — during the DNB fitness interview — that governance starts at the top.

SAFEST Pillars
How We Meet Each SAFEST Pillar
Each pillar maps directly to statutory requirements. Click to expand the full requirement chain.
S — Soundness (Deugdelijkheid)
17 Critical8 Important

The AI must be technically robust, validated, and resilient. It cannot introduce systemic risk into the payment infrastructure.

Primary statutes: DORA Art. 5-16, 25-27, 28(3) EU AI Act Art. 9-10, 15 Wft §3:17 PSD2 Art. 5(1)(e)
Key deliverables: AI system inventory, model validation reports, stress test results, DORA Register of Information (116 validation rules), fallback procedures, data quality framework, RTO documentation.
A — Accountability (Verantwoordelijkheid)
13 Critical5 Important

A human must remain in the loop. Individuals — not algorithms — are accountable for regulatory compliance.

Primary statutes: EU AI Act Art. 14, 72-73 GDPR Art. 22 DORA Art. 17-23 Wft §3:17 Wwft Art. 2d
Key deliverables: AI Governance Framework, 3LoD model, RACI matrix, human-in-the-loop design, kill switches, decision audit trail, incident response plan, DORA incident reporting templates.
F — Fairness (Eerlijkheid)
11 Critical7 Important

AI must not discriminate. Proactive testing and continuous monitoring across all protected characteristics.

Primary statutes: EU AI Act Art. 10 GDPR Art. 22 Wft §4:24a Dutch Equal Treatment Act EBA GL/2020/06
Key deliverables: Bias test results, fairness metrics, proxy variable analysis, training data representativeness, production fairness monitoring, use-case-specific fairness assessments (payments, credit, KYC, AML).
E — Ethics (Ethiek)
6 Critical5 Important

AI should align with societal values and not exploit vulnerable consumers.

Primary statutes: EU AI Act Art. 5 (prohibitions) Wft §4:24a EU Accessibility Act
Key deliverables: Board-approved ethics principles, prohibited AI uses list, vulnerable consumer safeguards, dark pattern audit, FRIA (for high-risk AI from Aug 2026).
S — Skills (Vaardigheden)
15 Critical4 Important

Board and staff must genuinely understand the AI. You cannot outsource the understanding. Most common area where FinTech applicants fall short.

Primary statutes: Wft §3:15 (two-person rule) Wft §3:8 (fitness) Wft §3:9 (propriety) EU AI Act Art. 4 (AI literacy) EBA GL/2019/02
Key deliverables: Policymaker CVs + interview preparation, geschiktheidstoets mock sessions, AI literacy programme, training records, team qualifications, vendor understanding documentation, succession plans.
T — Transparency (Transparantie)
11 Critical7 Important

You must explain how the AI reached every decision — to DNB, to customers, and internally.

Primary statutes: EU AI Act Art. 13, 50, Annex IV GDPR Art. 13-14, 22 DORA Art. 6(5) Wwft Art. 16
Key deliverables: Explainability documentation (SHAP/LIME), model cards, customer disclosure templates, adverse decision explanations, appeal/redress mechanism, decision traceability system, monitoring dashboards.

Product Management
Product Manager — Jobs-to-be-Done
What the Head of Product must deliver in each phase. Framed as JTBD statements with measurable outcomes.

Phase 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1-4)

Job 1.1
When we begin the licensing programme, I need to establish a complete AI system inventory so that every AI component is classified by risk level and mapped to statutory obligations.
Done when: Board-approved inventory exists with risk classification per EU AI Act Art. 6, and every system is linked to a SAFEST pillar owner.
Job 1.2
When forming the governance structure, I need to secure board approval for the AI Governance Framework so that accountability is established from day one.
Done when: Board resolution passed, 3LoD model documented, named AI-accountable board member, AI committee ToR signed.
Job 1.3
When assessing licensing scope, I need to produce a licensing boundary analysis so that DNB receives a clear, legally grounded explanation of which activities fall under which license.
Done when: Boundary document reviewed by legal counsel, covering PSD2/MiCAR/EMD2 scenarios with statutory references.
Job 1.4
When confirming board composition, I need to verify the two-person rule is met so that the Wft §3:15 prerequisite cannot block our application.
Done when: ≥2 policymakers appointed, CVs prepared, geschiktheidstoets preparation plan agreed.

Phase 2 — Core Policies (Weeks 3-8)

Job 2.1
When building the compliance backbone, I need to deliver DORA-aligned ICT risk management policies so that all AI systems have documented fallback procedures, RTOs, and incident response plans.
Done when: ICT risk policy covers all AI systems; incident response plan includes DORA 4h/72h/1mo timelines; tabletop exercise scheduled.
Job 2.2
When designing human oversight, I need to ensure every high-risk AI decision has a documented human-in-the-loop design so that no AI system operates without override capability.
Done when: HITL design doc per high-risk system, kill switch tested, automation bias training planned.
Job 2.3
When managing third-party AI risk, I need to create a vendor management policy and begin populating the DORA Register of Information so that we can demonstrate control over the AI supply chain.
Done when: Vendor policy approved; Register template populated with ≥80% of current AI/ML vendors; exit strategies drafted for critical functions.
Job 2.4
When rolling out AI literacy, I need to launch an organization-wide training programme so that EU AI Act Art. 4 obligations are met and training records exist for DNB.
Done when: Programme launched, ≥90% completion rate for mandatory modules, assessment pass records stored.

Phase 3 — Build & Test (Weeks 6-14)

Job 3.1
When generating technical evidence, I need to coordinate independent model validation for every AI system so that second-line validation reports exist before the DNB meeting.
Done when: Validation reports signed off by independent reviewer for each in-scope model. Findings tracked with remediation status.
Job 3.2
When testing for fairness, I need to deliver bias testing results across all protected characteristics so that DNB receives quantified evidence of non-discrimination.
Done when: Bias test reports per model with fairness metrics, proxy analysis, and intersectional results. Remediation actions documented.
Job 3.3
When building explainability, I need to implement and document XAI approaches per model so that any AI decision can be explained to DNB, customers, and internal stakeholders.
Done when: SHAP/LIME/equivalent implemented; global + local explanations demonstrated; audience-appropriate formats created.
Job 3.4
When completing the DORA Register, I need to finalize the Register of Information and run internal validation so that all 116 DNB validation rules pass.
Done when: Register in XBRL/XML format; internal dry-run passes completeness, consistency, format, and business logic checks.

Phase 4 — Evidence Package (Weeks 12-16)

Job 4.1
When assembling the application, I need to compile the complete evidence package (20 documents) so that every critical SAFEST item has substantive proof attached.
Done when: All 20 documents listed in "Documents to Bring to DNB" section are complete, reviewed, and cross-referenced to SAFEST items.
Job 4.2
When assessing readiness, I need to run the SAFEST self-assessment using the YAML checklist so that we have an objective score to determine if we should schedule the DNB meeting.
Done when: ≥80% of critical items (≥59 of 73) marked as complete with evidence references. Readiness level = "Ready".

Phase 5 — Pre-Application (Weeks 16-20)

Job 5.1
When preparing policymakers for DNB, I need to coordinate mock geschiktheidstoets sessions so that each policymaker can articulate AI strategy, risks, and governance under interview conditions.
Done when: Each policymaker completes ≥2 mock interviews; performance documented; gaps addressed.
Job 5.2
When engaging DNB, I need to prepare the pre-application meeting brief and anticipated Q&A responses so that the meeting demonstrates governance maturity and statutory awareness.
Done when: Meeting brief delivered; responses prepared for all 11 common DNB questions; designated speakers assigned per topic.

Phase 6 — Sustain (Post-licensing)

Job 6.1
When operating under license, I need to maintain continuous monitoring and annual revalidation cycles so that compliance evidence remains current and DNB supervisory expectations are met.
Done when: Annual model revalidation schedule operational; fairness monitoring live; DORA Register updated quarterly; regulatory reporting automated.
Job 6.2
When EU AI Act high-risk obligations take effect (Aug 2026), I need to ensure conformity assessment readiness so that our high-risk AI systems comply from day one.
Done when: Annex IV technical documentation complete; FRIA conducted; conformity assessment process defined.

Key Programme Risks
Risks that could delay DNB authorization or require Board intervention.
#RiskImpactLikelihoodMitigation
R1 Policymaker fitness interview failure — DNB rejects one or both policymakers Critical Medium Phase 1: early appointment. Phase 5: mock interviews. Contingency: identify reserve policymaker candidates.
R2 DORA Register of Information fails validation — 116 rules are complex; silent failures possible Critical Medium Phase 3: internal dry-run validation tool. Early engagement with DNB Portal test environment if available.
