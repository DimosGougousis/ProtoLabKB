Phase 2 — Organizational Adoption

Enterprise Implementation

Organizational adoption guide for regulated FinTechs — addresses how to implement governance structurally, not just procedurally. Who owns decisions, how governance flows through engineering workflows, and what tooling enforces it automatically.

When to Use

Apply Enterprise Implementation guidance when:

  • Establishing AI governance for the first time
  • Preparing for regulatory licensing (DNB, ECB)
  • Scaling AI governance across multiple teams
  • Integrating governance into agile development processes
  • Selecting and implementing governance tooling

Target: FinTechs seeking DNB/ECB licensing as PSPs, EMIs, or CASPs under MiCAR.

12-Month Adoption Playbook

The phased approach to enterprise AI governance:

  1. Months 1-3: Foundation — establish governance committee, define roles, initial risk tiering
  2. Months 4-6: Pilot — implement on 2-3 high-visibility AI systems, refine processes
  3. Months 7-9: Scale — roll out to all limited/high-risk systems, train teams
  4. Months 10-12: Optimize — automate compliance checks, prepare for audit

Key Outputs

Enterprise Implementation delivers:

  • Governance charter — documented authority and decision rights
  • Integrated workflows — governance embedded in development processes
  • Risk-based approach — proportionate governance by risk tier
  • Automated tooling — compliance-as-code and continuous monitoring
  • Audit readiness — evidence and documentation for regulatory examination

Regulatory Context

  • DNB Licensing Requirements — Governance for PSPs and EMIs
  • ECB Supervisory Expectations — AI risk management in banking
  • MiCAR — Crypto-asset service provider governance
  • EU AI Act — Organizational-level risk management
  • DORA — ICT governance and incident management
