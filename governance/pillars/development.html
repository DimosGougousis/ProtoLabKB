Subsection 1 of 4

Eval-Driven Development

Write the eval suite before the feature. If the eval passes before you build, your eval is wrong. The AI equivalent of TDD.

Why it matters

Without evals, teams ship AI features that "seem to work" but degrade silently. Eval-driven development creates a contractual handoff from Discovery: every acceptance criterion becomes an automated eval that gates deployment.

How we assess this

Eval pyramid (unit → integration → system → acceptance) maps to C3 trace hierarchies. CI/CD pipeline pushes eval results via OTLP. RED/GREEN status derived from trace event outcomes.

Subsection 2 of 4

Bias & Fairness Testing

Systematic testing across protected characteristics using multiple fairness metrics. Not a one-time check — continuous through the dev cycle.

Why it matters

EU AI Act requires documented fairness testing for high-risk systems. SAFEST Framework items F-01 and F-02 mandate bias identification and fairness metrics. A single metric isn't enough — different metrics can contradict each other.

How we assess this

5 fairness metrics (demographic parity, equalized odds, predictive parity, calibration, individual fairness) tested across all protected characteristics. C6 captures bias guardrail triggers, C5 tracks fairness drift. Results stored in safest_items table.

Subsection 3 of 4

Pre-Deployment Quality Gates

The must-pass checklist before any AI system goes to production. Every item has a verification method and evidence requirement.

Why it matters

Production incidents are 10-100x more expensive than catching issues in development. The pre-deployment gate is the last checkpoint before real users are affected. For high-risk systems, this gate requires Committee + Board sponsor sign-off.

How we assess this

Structured YAML checklist with 14 gate items. Agent pushes full C2 + C3 + C5 payloads at gate time. Gate approval recorded in audit_log table. Approval stored as evidence package for EU AI Act Article 11 documentation.

Subsection 4 of 4

Model Cards & Documentation

Auto-generated documentation combining agent metadata, performance baselines, cost profile, and limitations. Satisfies EU AI Act Article 11 requirements.

Why it matters

EU AI Act Article 11 mandates technical documentation for high-risk systems. Model cards provide a standardized format covering intended use, limitations, performance characteristics, and ethical considerations. Without automation, documentation drifts from reality.

How we assess this

Auto-generated from C1 (identity/scope), C4 (cost profile), C5 (performance baselines). Stored in article_11_documentation table. Regenerated on each model version promotion (DEV → STAGING → PROD).
