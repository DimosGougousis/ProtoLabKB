Drift Detection

Four drift types — data, concept, prompt, and tool-use — detected through statistical tests and continuous monitoring. Severity-classified responses with defined SLAs.

Why it matters

AI systems degrade silently. Input distributions shift, user behavior changes, prompts lose effectiveness, and tool APIs evolve. Without drift detection, you discover degradation from user complaints — or worse, regulatory audits.

How we assess this

C5 carries drift type, statistical test, score, severity, and per-feature breakdown. Detection cadence: daily for high-risk, weekly for limited, monthly for minimal. 7 statistical tests (PSI, KL Divergence, KS Test, etc.) across 4 drift categories.

Subsection 2 of 4

Incident Response

Standardized 12-section incident reports with reasoning trace analysis, root cause investigation, and DORA-compliant deadline tracking.

Why it matters

DORA mandates ICT incident reporting within 4 hours (initial), 72 hours (intermediate), and 1 month (final). EU AI Act Article 73 requires notification for serious incidents involving high-risk AI. Without structured incident management, deadlines are missed.

How we assess this

Auto-created when C6 severity thresholds crossed or C7 anomalous decisions detected. C3 provides reasoning traces for root cause analysis. C2 health state at incident time provides context. Deadline tracking with countdown timers.

Subsection 3 of 4

SAFEST Compliance

DNB's 6-pillar framework: Soundness, Accountability, Fairness, Ethics, Skills, Transparency. 112 compliance items mapped to data contracts for automated tracking.

Why it matters

Dutch Central Bank (DNB) guidelines require financial institutions to demonstrate AI governance across 6 pillars. Manual compliance tracking is error-prone and audit-unfriendly. Automated mapping from data contracts provides continuous, evidence-backed compliance.

How we assess this

Each SAFEST item maps to one or more contracts: S items ← C2+C5, A items ← C7+C3, F items ← C6+C5, E items ← C6+C1, S(kills) ← C1+manual, T items ← C3+C1. Stored in safest_items table. Automated items update on contract data arrival; manual items updated quarterly.

Subsection 4 of 4

EU AI Act & DORA Mapping

Automated regulatory compliance mapping. Article 11 documentation auto-generated. DORA incident deadlines tracked. Dutch Algorithm Register readiness assessed.

Why it matters

Regulatory deadlines are non-negotiable. EU AI Act fines reach 7% of global turnover. DORA requires ICT incident reporting within hours. Manual compliance tracking fails at scale. The platform auto-generates evidence and tracks deadlines.

How we assess this

C1 risk tier determines applicable Articles. C3 traces + C5 quality data auto-generate Article 11 documentation (5 elements). C7 decisions feed Annex III evidence. DORA deadlines triggered by incident creation. Compliance gap analysis runs on T2 CAIO dashboard (5-min refresh).
