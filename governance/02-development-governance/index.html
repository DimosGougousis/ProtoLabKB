Pillar 2

Development Governance

Ensures AI systems are built responsibly with systematic testing for quality, safety, and fairness before production. Core methodology is eval-driven development — the AI equivalent of test-driven development.

When to Use

Apply Development Governance during the build phase of any AI initiative:

  • When implementing AI/ML models or LLM-based features
  • When setting up continuous integration for AI systems
  • Before deploying any model to production
  • When updating or retraining existing models
  • When integrating third-party AI components

Trigger: All AI code must pass the eval-gate before deployment — no exceptions.

Who Is Responsible

RoleResponsibility
AI/ML EngineerResponsible — builds eval suites, implements quality gates, ensures bias testing is complete
Model OwnerAccountable — approves deployment based on eval results, signs off on pre-deployment gate
Product ManagerConsulted — validates acceptance criteria are met, confirms business requirements
Compliance Officer (2nd Line)Consulted — reviews bias testing results, validates fairness metrics
AI Governance CommitteeApprover — grants deployment approval for limited and high-risk systems

Key Output

Every AI system that passes through Development Governance exits with:

  1. Automated evaluation suite — comprehensive tests for quality, safety, fairness
  2. Bias testing results — documented fairness metrics across protected groups
  3. Pre-deployment approval — signed-off gate checklist
  4. CI/CD integration — eval-gate blocking automated deployments on failure

Regulatory Basis

  • EU AI Act Article 10 — Data governance and training, validation, testing datasets
  • EU AI Act Article 15 — Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity requirements
  • GDPR Article 25 — Data protection by design and by default
  • DNB Good Practice for AI — Model development and validation expectations
  • SAFEST items D-01 through D-12 — Development phase governance requirements
