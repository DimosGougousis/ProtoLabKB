Pillar 3

Runtime Governance

Ensures AI systems behave correctly, safely, and fairly while serving real users in production. Critical for agentic AI systems where autonomous agents make decisions and take actions.

When to Use

Apply Runtime Governance when AI systems are live and serving users:

  • When deploying AI systems to production environments
  • When operating autonomous or semi-autonomous AI agents
  • When implementing multi-agent workflows with delegation
  • When monitoring for safety violations or policy breaches
  • When responding to real-time incidents or anomalies

Trigger: All production AI systems must have runtime guardrails and continuous evaluation active.

Key Output

Every production AI system must have:

  1. Active guardrails — input/output filtering and safety policy enforcement
  2. Continuous evaluation — real-time monitoring of behavior and performance
  3. Control mechanisms — kill switch, circuit breaker, rollback capabilities
  4. Audit trail — complete logging of decisions and actions for accountability

Regulatory Basis

  • EU AI Act Article 14 — Human oversight requirements for high-risk AI
  • EU AI Act Article 15 — Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity in operation
  • DORA Article 23 — ICT-related incident management
  • DNB Good Practice for AI — Ongoing monitoring and risk management
  • SAFEST items R-01 through R-10 — Runtime governance requirements
