Cross-Cutting Concerns

Governance artifacts that span all four governance pillars — shared vocabulary, role definitions, regulatory mappings, and tooling context that other governance documents depend on.

When to Use

Reference Cross-Cutting Concerns throughout all governance activities:

  • When defining roles and responsibilities for AI governance
  • When interpreting regulatory requirements across frameworks
  • When selecting and evaluating governance tools
  • When establishing consistent terminology across teams
  • When mapping SAFEST items to implementation activities

Note: These artifacts provide the foundation that all other pillars build upon.

Key Purpose

Cross-Cutting Concerns provide:

  1. Shared vocabulary — consistent definitions everyone uses
  2. Clear accountability — defined roles and responsibilities
  3. Regulatory clarity — mapped requirements across frameworks
  4. Tool guidance — evaluated options for implementation
  5. Traceability — SAFEST items linked to implementation

Regulatory Coverage

  • EU AI Act — Comprehensive mapping of all articles to governance activities
  • DORA — ICT risk management and incident reporting requirements
  • GDPR — Data protection principles and processing requirements
  • PSD2 — Payment service requirements where applicable
  • DNB Guidelines — Dutch regulatory expectations for AI
  • SAFEST Framework — All 114 items mapped to implementation
