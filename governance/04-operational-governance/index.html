Pillar 4

Operational Governance

Ensures AI systems remain safe, secure, performant, compliant, and accountable over their entire production lifetime. Covers long-term concerns from deployment through decommissioning.

When to Use

Apply Operational Governance throughout the entire lifecycle of production AI systems:

  • When managing security threats and vulnerabilities
  • When responding to incidents or anomalies
  • When detecting and addressing model drift
  • When preparing for audits or regulatory inspections
  • When planning system retirement or decommissioning

Trigger: All production AI systems require continuous operational governance from deployment to decommissioning.

Key Output

Operational Governance ensures:

  1. Security posture — defenses against emerging threats and vulnerabilities
  2. Incident readiness — documented procedures and trained response teams
  3. Drift awareness — continuous monitoring with automated revalidation triggers
  4. Audit readiness — complete documentation and evidence for regulatory inspections
  5. Lifecycle management — planned retirement and transition strategies

Regulatory Basis

  • EU AI Act Article 12 — Record-keeping and logging requirements
  • EU AI Act Article 13 — Transparency and provision of information
  • DORA Article 23-27 — ICT-related incident management and reporting
  • GDPR Articles 32-33 — Security of processing and breach notification
  • DNB Good Practice for AI — Ongoing monitoring and model risk management
  • SAFEST items O-01 through O-15 — Operational governance requirements
