People Strategy — Engineers Are More Precious, Not Less
The load-bearing section · If you only nail one part of the conversation, nail this one
Companion narrative: memoized-questing-sphinx.md §4
Frame engineers as amplified, not bypassed
Open with empathy, not argument. "I know the existing technical engineers are not in favour of the project, and I think their resistance is reasonable — most AI projects they've seen pitched probably did treat them as the cost line to be removed. So I'd start by acknowledging that, not arguing with it." — Then earn the right to make your case.
↻
The Reframe — Why They Become More Valuable
say these out loud
01From the boring 80% → to the strategic 20%
Today their hours go on standard CNC aluminum brackets, IM parts with no surprises. AI handles those. What's left for them: novel geometries, exotic materials, customer DFM consulting, edge cases that protect Protolabs' reputation. That's the work they actually trained for.
02From quote calculators → to teachers of the system
Every override they make is labeled training data. The model gets better because they correct it. We make this visible — a dashboard showing "Engineer X's overrides improved next-month accuracy by Y bps." This is a status upgrade, not a downgrade.
03From optional reviewers → to legally required oversight
Under the EU AI Act, anything touching safety-critical components (medical, aerospace) requires human oversight. We're in the Netherlands. That oversight is regulation, not preference — it gives them institutional permanence. We cannot operate without them. Put it in writing.
✓
Operational Moves — Make It Real, Not Lip Service
commit to these on day one
  • 1
    Co-design, not roll-outTwo of the order engineers sit on the design team as voting members, not advisors. They define the override UX, the confidence thresholds, the kill-switches. No model ships without their sign-off.
  • 2
    Weekly office hours / pairingAI engineers and order engineers walk through last week's edge cases together. Both sides learn. Builds shared language; kills the "us vs them" frame.
  • 3
    Promote one of themFind the most respected, most curious order engineer. Make them the bridge — "AI Engineering Liaison" or similar. Their endorsement is worth ten of mine.
  • 4
    Metrics that celebrate them"Engineer save rate" (% of low-confidence flags where engineer prevented a bad quote). "Engineer-driven model improvement" (override → accuracy gain). These show up in QBRs.
  • 5
    No headcount cut tied to this project — publiclyThe win is throughput growth at the same staffing, not staff reduction. Any future headcount conversation is separate, never bundled in. Say it on day one and don't walk it back.

When They Push Back — One-Line Anchors
memorize these — they'll come up
What they (or Teresa) might say Your anchor response Why it lands
"We've heard 'AI will help you' before. It always means we lose headcount." "I'm not asking you to trust the slogan — I'm asking you to trust the structure. No headcount cut is bundled into this project, in writing. If we ever revisit headcount, it's a separate conversation." Concedes the cynicism is earned; offers a concrete commitment, not a promise
"The AI will get it wrong on a strategic customer and we'll be left explaining." "That's exactly why strategic accounts and novel geometries route to a human by default. Confidence-based routing means the AI never gets near the orders where being wrong is unrecoverable." Architecture, not assurance — answers the actual technical worry
"We don't have time to teach a model AND do our day jobs." "You won't be teaching it on top — the override IS your day job. The system learns from the work you'd do anyway. The only new ask is letting us instrument it." Removes the perceived workload tax; makes adoption frictionless
"What happens to the junior engineers who learn by doing the easy quotes?" "Real concern. Career path needs explicit redesign — juniors shadow on edge cases earlier, do AI override-grading as training, rotate through the AI team. Loop the L&D team in from day one." Shows you've thought past the surface; signals you take growth pipelines seriously
"You're not even from manufacturing — why should we trust you on this?" "You shouldn't, yet. Trust gets earned in the first 30 days of shadowing without proposing anything. If I haven't earned it by day 60, that's a real signal and I want to hear it directly." Disarms with humility; sets a measurable bar for your own performance
"This is a Teresa pet project. Once she moves on, the AI gets dropped on us." "That's why governance matters more than enthusiasm. The architecture is high-risk-ready by design — it survives leadership turnover because the audit trail and human oversight are regulatory requirements, not management preferences." Acknowledges org politics without dismissing them; ties durability to compliance
The line to repeat — in the room and after
"You're not being bypassed. You're being amplified — and you're becoming the people who decide what good looks like."
