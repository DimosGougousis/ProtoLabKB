# EU AI Act Governance for Manufacturing AI Systems

## Risk Classification (Article 6)

### High-Risk AI Systems
AI systems that pose significant risks to health, safety, or fundamental rights, including:
- AI-assisted CNC programming with autonomous parameter setting
- Material substitution recommendations affecting product safety
- Predictive maintenance systems for critical manufacturing equipment
- Quality control vision systems making pass/fail decisions

### Prohibited AI Practices (Article 5)
- Social scoring of suppliers or employees
- Real-time biometric identification in workplace settings
- AI systems manipulating human behavior in unacceptable ways

## Transparency Requirements (Article 52)

### For High-Risk AI Systems
- **Disclosure**: Users must be informed when interacting with AI systems
- **Explanation**: Ability to understand AI outputs and decision-making processes
- **Human Oversight**: Humans must retain ability to override AI decisions
- **Accuracy and Robustness**: Systems must perform reliably under specified conditions

## Data Governance (Article 10)

### Training Data Requirements
- **Documentation**: Complete records of data sources and preprocessing
- **Representativeness**: Training data must reflect real-world conditions
- **Bias Mitigation**: Procedures to detect and address algorithmic bias
- **Quality Assurance**: Data validation and error-checking procedures

## Technical Documentation (Article 11)

### Required Content
- **System Description**: Purpose, intended use, and operational context
- **Algorithm Details**: Training methods, data used, and performance metrics
- **Risk Assessment**: Identified risks and mitigation measures
- **Human Oversight**: Procedures for human intervention and override
- **Change Management**: Version control and update procedures

## Post-Market Monitoring (Article 72)

### Continuous Obligations
- **Performance Monitoring**: Track system performance in real-world use
- **Incident Reporting**: Report serious incidents and system failures
- **Data Collection**: Gather feedback on system effectiveness
- **Updates**: Implement improvements based on monitoring results

## Manufacturing-Specific Applications

### CNC Process Optimization
- **Parameter Setting**: AI recommendations for feeds, speeds, tool paths
- **Material Selection**: Automated material substitution based on availability/cost
- **Quality Prediction**: ML models predicting defect likelihood
- **Maintenance Scheduling**: Predictive analytics for tool and equipment maintenance

### Governance Requirements
- **Human-in-the-Loop**: Critical decisions require human approval
- **Audit Trails**: Complete logging of AI decisions and human overrides
- **Bias Testing**: Regular validation of AI recommendations against human expertise
- **Fallback Procedures**: Manual processes when AI systems fail

## Implementation Timeline
- **February 2025**: Prohibitions on unacceptable AI practices
- **February 2026**: Transparency obligations for general-purpose AI
- **February 2027**: Full requirements for high-risk AI systems

## Compliance Verification
- **Conformity Assessment**: Third-party evaluation for high-risk systems
- **CE Marking**: Required for AI systems placed on EU market
- **Market Surveillance**: Ongoing monitoring by national authorities
- **Penalties**: Fines up to 6% of global turnover for non-compliance
