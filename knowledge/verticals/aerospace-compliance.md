# Aerospace Compliance Requirements

## Overview

Aerospace manufacturing demands the highest levels of quality, traceability, and regulatory compliance. ProtoLabs maintains comprehensive certifications and processes to meet the stringent requirements of aerospace and defense customers, from commercial aviation to space applications.

---

## AS9100D Aerospace Quality Management

### Key Requirements

AS9100D is the international quality management system standard for the aerospace industry, built upon ISO 9001:2015 with additional aerospace-specific requirements.

| Requirement Area | AS9100D Specifics | ISO 9001 Base |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Configuration Management | Mandatory documented process | Not required |
| Counterfeit Parts Prevention | Specific controls required | Not addressed |
| First Article Inspection | AS9102 FAI required | Not required |
| Key Characteristics | Identification and control required | Not required |
| Product Safety | Specific requirements | General safety |
| Risk Management | Enhanced risk-based thinking | Basic risk |

### Differences from ISO 9001

1. **Enhanced Configuration Management**: Complete traceability of design changes throughout product lifecycle
2. **Counterfeit Parts Prevention**: Mandatory supplier controls and part authentication
3. **First Article Inspection**: Structured FAI process per AS9102 standard
4. **Key Characteristics**: Identification and statistical control of critical features
5. **Product Safety**: Specific processes to address safety-critical requirements

## FAR 25 (Federal Aviation Regulations) Compliance

### Overview

FAR Part 25 contains airworthiness standards for transport category airplanes. While primarily applicable to aircraft manufacturers, suppliers of aircraft parts must ensure compliance with applicable sections.

### Key Requirements for Parts Suppliers

| FAR Section | Requirement | ProtoLabs Relevance |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| 25.603 | Materials | Material specifications and certifications |
| 25.605 | Fabrication Methods | Process validation and control |
| 25.607 | Fasteners | Hardware specifications |
| 25.609 | Protection of Structure | Finish specifications |
| 25.613 | Material Strength Properties | Mechanical property testing |

### Material Specifications

Aerospace materials must meet strict specifications:

- **Metals**: AMS (Aerospace Material Specifications) standards
- **Plastics**: RTCA DO-160 or equivalent flammability requirements
- **Composites**: Specific qualification testing per OEM requirements

### Documentation Requirements

1. **Material Certifications**: Mill test reports with chemical and mechanical properties
2. **Process Certifications**: NADCAP for special processes (see below)
3. **Test Reports**: Mechanical testing, NDT results
4. **Traceability**: Lot numbers, serial numbers as required

## NADCAP Special Processes

### Overview

NADCAP (National Aerospace and Defense Contractors Accreditation Program) provides accreditation for special processes in aerospace manufacturing. ProtoLabs works with NADCAP-accredited suppliers for processes requiring this certification.

### NADCAP Special Process Categories

| Process Category | NADCAP Code | ProtoLabs Application |
|------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Heat Treating | HT | Stress relieving, hardening |
| Welding | WT | Component joining |
| Non-Destructive Testing | NDT | Inspection and validation |
| Chemical Processing | CP | Surface treatments, passivation |
| Coatings | CT | Protective finishes |
| Materials Testing | MT | Mechanical testing |
| Conventional Machining | CM | Precision machining |

### Heat Treatment (HT)

**Processes Requiring NADCAP HT**:
- Solution heat treatment
- Aging and precipitation hardening
- Stress relieving
- Annealing

**Common Aerospace Applications**:
- Aluminum alloys (6061-T6, 7075-T73)
- Titanium alloys (Ti-6Al-4V)
- Stainless steels (17-4 PH, 15-5 PH)

### Welding (WT)

**NADCAP Welding Processes**:
- GTAW (TIG) welding
- GMAW (MIG) welding
- Electron beam welding
- Laser beam welding
- Resistance welding

**ProtoLabs Approach**:
ProtoLabs does not perform welding in-house but works with NADCAP-accredited welding suppliers for aerospace projects requiring welded components.

### Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

**NADCAP NDT Methods**:

| Method | Code | Application |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Liquid Penetrant | PT | Surface crack detection |
| Magnetic Particle | MT | Ferromagnetic material defects |
| Radiographic | RT | Internal defect detection |
| Ultrasonic | UT | Internal flaw detection |
| Eddy Current | ET | Surface and near-surface defects |
| Visual Inspection | VT | Surface condition assessment |

**ProtoLabs NDT Capabilities**:
- Visual inspection (in-house)
- Dimensional inspection (CMM)
- Surface finish measurement
- External NDT services through accredited partners

### Chemical Processing (CP)

**NADCAP Chemical Processes**:
- Passivation (stainless steel)
- Anodizing (aluminum)
- Conversion coating
- Chemical milling
- Etching
- Cleaning

**Surface Treatment Applications**:
- Corrosion resistance
- Paint adhesion
- Electrical insulation
- Wear resistance

## Counterfeit Parts Prevention

### Overview

Counterfeit parts pose a significant risk to aerospace safety and mission success. AS9100D and FAA regulations require robust counterfeit parts prevention programs.

### Risk Sources

| Risk Category | Examples | Mitigation |
|--------------|----------|------------|
| Unauthorized suppliers | Brokers, independent distributors | Approved supplier lists only |
| Suspect counterfeit markings | False lot numbers, fake certs | Supplier audits, testing |
| Previously used parts | Refurbished as new | Inspection, traceability |
| Non-conforming materials | Substandard substitutions | Material testing, certs |

### Supplier Controls

**Approved Supplier Requirements**:
1. AS9100 or ISO 9001 certification
2. Traceability systems
3. Counterfeit parts prevention procedures
4. Certificate of Conformance for all shipments
5. Material certifications with mill test reports

**Supplier Audit Program**:
- Initial qualification audits
- Periodic surveillance audits
- Special audits for cause
- Focus on high-risk suppliers

### Part Authenticity Verification

**Verification Methods**:

| Method | Application | ProtoLabs Capability |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Visual inspection | Markings, packaging | In-house |
| Dimensional inspection | Critical dimensions | CMM, in-house |
| Material testing | Composition, properties | External labs |
| XRF analysis | Material verification | External partners |
| Documentation review | Certs, traceability | In-house |

### Documentation Requirements

1. **Certificate of Conformance**: Supplier certification of compliance
2. **Material Certifications**: Mill test reports with chemical/mechanical properties
3. **Test Reports**: Inspection and test results
4. **Traceability Records**: Lot numbers, serial numbers
5. **Chain of Custody**: Documentation of handling and storage

## Configuration Management for Aerospace

### Overview

Configuration management ensures that aerospace products conform to approved designs and that changes are controlled throughout the product lifecycle.

### Configuration Management Elements

| Element | Description | ProtoLabs Implementation |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|
| Configuration Identification | Unique identification of items | Part numbers, revision levels |
| Configuration Control | Change management process | ECO process, customer approval |
| Configuration Status Accounting | Records of configuration | Build records, as-built configs |
| Configuration Audits | Verification of conformance | FAI, inspection records |

### Design Control Requirements

**AS9100D Design and Development Controls**:
1. Design planning
2. Design inputs (requirements)
3. Design outputs (specifications)
4. Design review
5. Design verification
6. Design validation
7. Design changes (control)
8. Design records

### Change Management Process

**Engineering Change Order (ECO) Process**:

| Step | Action | Responsibility |
|------|--------|----------------|
1. | Change request submitted | Customer/Engineering |
2. | Impact assessment | ProtoLabs Engineering |
3. | Customer approval | Customer |
4. | Implementation | ProtoLabs Production |
5. | Verification | Quality Assurance |
6. | Documentation update | Document Control |

### Revision Control

**Part Numbering and Revision**:
- Base part number format: [Process]-[Material]-[Serial]
- Revision levels: -A, -B, -C, etc.
- Customer part numbers: Cross-referenced in system
- Drawing revisions: Controlled per AS9102

## First Article Inspection (FAI) Requirements

### Overview

First Article Inspection (FAI) is a critical aerospace requirement that verifies production processes can manufacture parts conforming to engineering requirements.

### AS9102 FAI Standard

ProtoLabs follows AS9102 (Aerospace First Article Inspection Requirement) for all aerospace FAI activities.

### When FAI is Required

| Scenario | FAI Requirement |
|----------|-----------------|
| New part introduction | Full FAI required |
| Design change affecting form/fit/function | Full or partial FAI |
| Manufacturing process change | Partial FAI |
| Tooling change (new/repaired) | Partial FAI |
| > 2 years since last production | Full FAI |
| Change of manufacturing location | Full FAI |

### FAI Report Components

**AS9102 Forms**:

1. **Form 1: Part Number Accountability**
   - Part number and revision
   - Part name and description
   - Drawing number and revision
   - Additional changes
   - Manufacturing process reference

2. **Form 2: Product Accountability - Materials, Specifications and Processes**
   - Material specifications
   - Special processes (heat treat, plating, etc.)
   - Process specifications
   - Supplier information

3. **Form 3: Characteristic Accountability, Verification and Compatibility Evaluation**
   - Drawing characteristics (dimensions, tolerances)
   - Measurement results
   - Inspection method
   - Acceptance criteria
   - Measured values

### Critical Characteristics

**Key Characteristic Identification**:
- Safety-critical dimensions
- Fit and interface dimensions
- Performance-critical features
- Material properties
- Surface finish requirements

**Statistical Requirements**:
- Critical characteristics require statistical process control
- Cpk ≥ 1.33 for critical characteristics
- Cpk ≥ 1.67 for safety-critical characteristics

### FAI Documentation Package

Complete FAI package includes:
1. AS9102 Forms 1, 2, and 3
2. Drawing with ballooned characteristics
3. Material certifications
4. Special process certifications (NADCAP)
5. Test reports
6. Inspection records
7. Photo documentation (as required)

## ITAR/EAR Reminder for Defense-Related Aerospace

### Overview

Defense-related aerospace components are subject to strict export control regulations. ProtoLabs maintains compliance programs for both ITAR and EAR requirements.

### ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations)

**Registration**: M26924 (valid through 2026)

**Applicability**:
- Defense articles on USML Category VIII (aircraft) and XV (spacecraft)
- Technical data related to defense articles
- Defense services

**ITAR-Controlled Manufacturing**:

| Capability | ITAR Status | Facility |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| DMLS 3D Printing | ✅ ITAR Compliant | North Carolina |
| CNC Machining | ✅ ITAR Compliant | Minnesota, New Hampshire |
| Injection Molding | ✅ ITAR Compliant | Minnesota |

**ITAR Requirements for Customers**:
1. Manufacturing must occur in ITAR-registered facility
2. No foreign national access to ITAR-controlled technical data
3. End-use certificates may be required
4. Secure handling and storage of controlled technical data
5. Registration with DDTC (Directorate of Defense Trade Controls)

### EAR (Export Administration Regulations)

**Classification**: Most commercial aerospace parts fall under EAR99

**Export Control Classification Numbers (ECCN)**:

| Category | Description | Typical ECCN |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Commercial aircraft parts | Standard components | EAR99 |
| Advanced materials | Specialized alloys | 1C002, 1C996 |
| Navigation systems | GPS/INS components | 7A003, 7A994 |
| Propulsion systems | Engine components | 9A001, 9A101 |

**Restricted Parties Screening**:
- Denied Persons List (DPL)
- Entity List
- Specially Designated Nationals (SDN)
- Unverified List (UVL)

### OFAC Sanctions Compliance

Compliance with U.S. Treasury Department sanctions programs:
- Country-specific embargoes (Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea)
- Sectoral sanctions (Russia, Venezuela)
- Specially Designated Nationals blocking

### Export Control Best Practices

1. **Classification Review**: Determine correct USML or ECCN classification
2. **License Determination**: Assess license requirements for destination/end-use
3. **Documentation**: Maintain export control records for 5 years
4. **Training**: Annual export control training for relevant personnel
5. **Screening**: Check all parties against restricted lists

## Documentation Requirements for Aerospace Parts

### Required Documentation Package

Every aerospace part order requires comprehensive documentation to ensure traceability and compliance:

#### 1. Engineering Documentation

| Document | Description | Retention |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Customer Drawing | Approved part drawing with revision | Permanent |
| CAD Model | Native or STEP file | Permanent |
| Specification | Material and process specifications | Permanent |
| Purchase Order | Customer PO with requirements | 10 years |

#### 2. Manufacturing Documentation

| Document | Description | Retention |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Router/Traveler | Manufacturing sequence | 10 years |
| Setup Sheets | Machine setup parameters | 10 years |
| Inspection Plan | Inspection requirements | 10 years |
| Work Instructions | Step-by-step procedures | Current revision |

#### 3. Quality Documentation

| Document | Description | Retention |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| AS9102 FAI Report | First Article Inspection | Permanent |
| Material Certification | Mill test report | Permanent |
| Special Process Certs | NADCAP certificates | Permanent |
| Inspection Records | Dimensional reports | 10 years |
| NDT Reports | Non-destructive testing | 10 years |
| Test Reports | Mechanical testing | 10 years |
| Certificate of Conformance | Compliance statement | 10 years |

#### 4. Traceability Documentation

| Document | Description | Retention |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Lot Numbers | Material lot traceability | Permanent |
| Serial Numbers | Part serialization | Permanent |
| Supplier Records | Supplier information | 10 years |
| Calibration Records | Equipment calibration | Life + 2 years |

### Documentation Control Requirements

1. **Document Approval**: All documents must be approved prior to use
2. **Document Distribution**: Controlled distribution to prevent obsolete document use
3. **Document Changes**: Formal change control with revision history
4. **External Documents**: Control of customer and regulatory documents
5. **Record Retention**: Minimum retention periods as specified above
6. **Record Legibility**: Records must remain legible throughout retention period
7. **Record Accessibility**: Records must be retrievable for review/audit

### Digital Documentation

ProtoLabs maintains digital documentation systems with:
- Secure cloud storage with backup
- Version control for all documents
- Access controls and audit trails
- Electronic signatures compliant with 21 CFR Part 11 (where applicable)
- Long-term preservation formats
