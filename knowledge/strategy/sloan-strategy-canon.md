# Sloan Strategy Canon

## Overview

The MIT Sloan School of Management has developed five complementary lenses for strategic analysis. Together, they provide a comprehensive framework for understanding competitive positioning and strategic options.

## The Five Lenses

| Lens | Core Question | Focus | Time Horizon |
|------|---------------|-------|--------------|
| **Delta Model** | How do we bond with customers? | Customer-centric positioning | 3-5 years |
| **Platform Leadership** | How do we orchestrate ecosystems? | Ecosystem architecture | 5-10 years |
| **Dynamic Capabilities** | How do we adapt to change? | Organizational agility | Ongoing |
| **Simple Rules** | What guides our decisions? | Strategic heuristics | Ongoing |
| **Transient Advantage** | How do we compete when advantages are temporary? | Continuous innovation | 1-3 years |

## Application to Protolabs

### Manufacturing Services Context
- **Delta Model**: Bonding through speed, quality, and expertise
- **Platform Leadership**: Digital manufacturing network ecosystem
- **Dynamic Capabilities**: Rapid process innovation, material expertise
- **Simple Rules**: "Quote in 24 hours", "DFM feedback always"
- **Transient Advantage**: Continuous process improvement, new technology adoption

### AI Strategy Context
- **Delta Model**: AI-powered customer experience differentiation
- **Platform Leadership**: AI services ecosystem for manufacturing
- **Dynamic Capabilities**: AI model development, data asset management
- **Simple Rules**: "Human review for regulated outputs", "Cost per quote < $X"
- **Transient Advantage**: Rapid AI capability deployment

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Frame**: Define strategic question and scope
2. **Apply Lenses**: Analyze through each of the five lenses
3. **Synthesize**: Identify patterns and tensions across lenses
4. **Generate Options**: Develop strategic alternatives
5. **Evaluate**: Assess options against lens-specific criteria
6. **Recommend**: Prioritize actions with clear rationale
